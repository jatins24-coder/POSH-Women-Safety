About POSH

The POSH (Prevention of Sexual Harassment) Act, 2013, was enacted to create a safe and inclusive environment for women at the workplace. Inspired by the Vishaka Guidelines of 1997, the law mandates preventive, prohibitive, and redressal measures for sexual harassment incidents.

Every employer is required to form an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC), conduct awareness workshops, and submit annual reports of complaints received and redressed.

Know Your Rights

For Employees

Every woman has the right to a safe, secure, and dignified workplace. File complaints without fear of retaliation.

For Employers

Establish an ICC, conduct regular training, maintain confidentiality, and ensure timely action on complaints.

For ICC Members

Investigate fairly, follow due process, and resolve issues within 90 days while maintaining privacy.

Report a Case

If you face or witness harassment, report it within 3 months. Provide written documentation to your ICC. Ensure facts are clear and timelines are noted. All complaints are to be resolved confidentially within 90 days.

FAQs

What is the POSH Act?
It is a law enacted in 2013 to prevent sexual harassment at the workplace for women in India.
Is POSH applicable in startups?
Yes. POSH applies to all organizations with more than 10 employees.
